About Skin Cancer

Skin cancer is the most common type of cancer, affecting millions of people worldwide each year. It occurs when skin cells grow uncontrollably and form a mass of tissue known as a tumor. If left untreated, skin cancer can spread to other parts of the body and become life-threatening.

Types of Skin Cancer

  • Basal cell carcinoma (BCC): The most common type, often appearing as a small, shiny bump or nodule on the skin.
  • Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC): The second most common type, which may appear as a firm red nodule, a flat lesion with a scaly crust, or a new sore that doesn't heal.
  • Melanoma: The most serious type of skin cancer, which can develop in an existing mole or appear as a new dark spot on the skin.

Facts about Skin Cancer

  • Skin cancer is the most common type of cancer in Canada, with thousands of new cases diagnosed every year.
  • Melanoma, though less common, is the most dangerous form of skin cancer and accounts for the majority of skin cancer-related deaths in Canada.
  • Early detection and treatment of skin cancer significantly improve the chances of successful outcomes.
  • Canadians, especially those with fair skin, are at higher risk of developing skin cancer due to UV exposure, even in northern climates.
